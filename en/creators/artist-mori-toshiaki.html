Mori Toshiaki

Mori Toshiaki is the eldest son of Mori Taiji, who carries on a lineage of the “Rokusho” (the six historic Bizen kiln families), and he is the 19th generation of the Mori family. Even before he could speak, he was already playing with clay. Through his father Taiji, he learned not only refined techniques but also the Mori family’s philosophy: that Bizen ware is not a tradition for artists, but a culture for the people.

He believes Bizen ware holds cultural value in enriching everyday life by embodying wabi-sabi—the Japanese aesthetic of accepting beauty within imperfection. Inspired by this cultural significance, he began studying cultural heritage to earn his bachelor’s degree.

Today, he adapts his artistic style to suit contemporary tastes and aims to share Japanese culture with the world through Bizen ware.

PROFILE

Biography

1982
Born in Imbe, Bizen City (born into the Mori family, a direct line of the historic “Rokusho” Bizen kiln families)
2005
Studied at the Bizen Ceramic Center
2006
Began producing works at Hozan-gama, a Bizen ware kiln
2007
Selected for the Okayama Prefectural Art Exhibition
(also selected in 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011, 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018, and 2023)
2009
Selected for the Chozaburo Prize Contemporary Ceramics Biennale (Aichi)
2010
Selected for the Contemporary Tea Ceramics Exhibition (Gifu) (also selected in 2012)
Selected for the Chugoku Branch Exhibition of the Japan Traditional Crafts Exhibition (also selected in 2012, 2016, 2018, 2020, and 2021)
2011
Selected for the Tabe Art Museum Grand Prize “The Art of Chanoyu: Form Exhibition” (Shimane)
Hosted the East Japan Reconstruction Support Charity event “from〇bizen” (held annually thereafter through 2019)
2013
Participated in JR West campaign “Ekikara × Bizen Ware”
2014
Formed “Bizen-yaki CARAVAN” with three Bizen artists; traveled across the United States while holding ceramic exchanges, talks, workshops, and exhibitions (San Francisco to New York)
・“Bizen in Iowa -A Clay Cultural Exchange-” The Ceramics Center (Cedar Rapids, Iowa, USA)
・“Bizen Pottery Artists from Japan” Sara Japanese Pottery (New York, USA)
2015
With six Bizen artists, held exhibitions, talks, and workshops in London and Okehampton (UK)
・“THE POWER OF BIZEN” Daiwa Anglo-Japanese Foundation Japan-house Gallery (London, UK)
・“JAPANESE POTTERS -the next generation-” Kigbeare Studios & Gallery (Okehampton, Devon, UK)
Participated as a member of the project to build a Bizen-style anagama kiln in Oxford University’s research forest: “Oxford Anagama Kiln Project (now Oxford University Kilns)”
2018
Created commemorative gifts for the 100th anniversary of diplomatic relations between Japan and Ecuador; presented to both governments
Invited artist for Artist-in-Residence Suzu (Suzu City, Ishikawa)
Hosted “Ceramic Art Bizen in Shizutani” (Former Shizutani School, Bizen City) (also held in 2019)
Joined a support project to help restore spirits after the West Japan floods (Mabi, Kurashiki), delivering Bizen ware vessels to affected areas
2020
Selected for the Tobi Exhibition (Tokyo) (also selected in 2023)
Served for one year as President of the Bizen Junior Chamber of Commerce
Organized art exchange exhibitions in Taiwan and Japan
・“Tai–Japan Art Exchange in Pingtung” (Pingtung, Taiwan)
・“Japan–Taiwan Art Exchange: ‘Ceramics’ and ‘Sculpture’” (Setouchi City, Okayama)
Served as a special lecturer for “Shizutani Studies,” a Bizen ware seminar at Wake Shizutani High School (Okayama) (ongoing)
2022
Presented work at London Craft Week (UK)
Appeared as a guest in an online discussion hosted by the Embassy of Japan in the UK: “Making Pottery in Bizen and the UK”
2023
Presented work at JAPAN EXPO in Paris (France)
Coordinated “UNESCO School in Bizen”
Invited children and students from eight countries and led Bizen ware workshops and instruction

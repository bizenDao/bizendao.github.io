Mori Toshiaki

Within the flickering interplay of Bizen clay and flame, Mori Toshiaki brings together stillness and tension. Valuing the "space" that blends naturally into daily life, he designs each vessel with careful attention to its center of gravity, the feel of the rim against the lips, and the way light falls across its surface. (Provisional — may be revised)

My Definition of Bizen Pottery

Bizen-yaki is completed through the convergence of the serendipity born from flame and the passage of time spent with its user. That is why I design each piece for everyday use — refining its balance, thickness, lip feel, and texture — while embracing the landscape painted by fire. I believe the true value lies in achieving both. (Provisional)
